# Specification Document: The Quant Forensic Research Terminal

**Project Vision:** To transition from a standard metrics dashboard to a high-fidelity, institutional-grade decision-analysis environment. This terminal focuses on forensic trade reconstruction, counter-factual simulation, and probabilistic robustness.

---

## 1. Architectural Philosophy
*   **Browser as Visualization Engine:** Data processing and heavy computation are offloaded to Web Workers and DuckDB-Wasm. The UI remains a high-performance shell.
*   **Forensic Density:** Priority is given to information density over "retail" whitespace. 
*   **Synchronized State:** Every panel is a different lens on the same "Snapshot." A selection in one panel propagates instantly to all others.
*   **Local-First / Privacy-Centric:** All analysis occurs on the client side using uploaded research snapshots (CSV/Parquet/JSON).

---

## 2. Technical Stack
*   **Framework:** Next.js (App Router) + TypeScript.
*   **Data Engine:** **DuckDB-Wasm** (for high-speed analytical SQL queries in-browser) + **Arquero** (for data wrangling).
*   **State Management:** **Zustand** (Global State) + **Custom Event Bus** (High-frequency crosshair/teleport events).
*   **Charts:** **TradingView Lightweight Charts** (Candlesticks/Execution) + **Observable Plot/D3** (Quant Distributions/MAE-MFE).
*   **Persistence:** **IndexedDB** (via Dexie.js) for caching large datasets between sessions.

---

## 3. Core Functional Modules

### 3.1. The Forensic Market Replay
*   **Dynamic Resolution:** Support for multi-timeframe (HTF) overlays on a single chart.
*   **Execution Markers:** High-fidelity markers for Entry, Exit, and Scaled-outs.
*   **Liquidity Visualization:** Overlay of historical order book depth or session volume profiles if provided in the dataset.
*   **The Teleport Engine:** (See Section 4).

### 3.2. The Trade Graveyard (Counter-Factual Analysis)
*   **Logic:** Visualization of trades that were generated by the "Alpha" but killed by the "Risk Filter."
*   **Ghost Rendering:** Rejected trades rendered at 30% opacity.
*   **Attribution Labels:** Hovering over a rejected trade reveals the specific filter hit (e.g., *“Rejected: ATR Volatility > 2.5”*).
*   **Opportunity Cost Metrics:** Automatic calculation of "Lost PnL" vs. "Saved Drawdown" from the graveyard.

### 3.3. Monte Carlo & Robustness Lab
*   **Resampling Engine:** Web-worker-based bootstrapping (10,000+ iterations).
*   **Visual Outputs:** 
    *   **Probability Cones:** Expected equity growth paths.
    *   **Drawdown Distributions:** Histogram of max drawdown across simulations.
    *   **Ruin Probability:** Calculator based on current strategy expectancy.
*   **Parameter Sweeps:** Heatmaps showing strategy performance across different lookback periods or threshold variables.

### 3.4. Regime & Feature Attribution
*   **Contextual Tagging:** Automatic categorization of market environments (Trending, Ranging, High-Vol, News-Heavy).
*   **Equity Curve Splitting:** View separate equity curves for "London Session" vs. "NY Session" or "Trending" vs. "Mean Reverting."
*   **MAE/MFE Scatter Plots:** Visualizing trade "Efficiency" (How much heat was taken vs. how much profit was left on the table).

## 4. The "Teleport" Specification
The **Teleport** feature is the primary navigation mechanism for forensic audit. It allows the researcher to move from an abstract data point (a row in a table) to a physical market moment (the candles).

### 4.1. The Trigger Mechanism
*   **Input:** User clicks a trade ID in the Trade Table, a point on the Equity Curve, or a "Ghost Trade" in the Graveyard.
*   **Action:** The system broadcasts a `TELEPORT_EVENT` containing:
    1.  `timestamp`
    2.  `price_range` (Entry to Exit)
    3.  `context_padding` (e.g., 50 candles before/after)
    4.  `timeframe_hint` (e.g., "M15")

### 4.2. The Execution Workflow
1.  **Coordinate Shift:** The Market Replay panel instantly scrolls to the target `timestamp`.
2.  **Smart Zoom:** The Y-axis (Price) and X-axis (Time) auto-scale to fit the entire duration of the trade within the viewport.
3.  **Overlay Activation:** The specific Trade Inspector for that ID opens in the sidebar, showing trade-specific "DNA" (MAE/MFE, indicators at time of entry).
4.  **Breadcrumb Tracking:** A "Return to Overview" floating button appears to restore the previous zoom level and global view.

## 5. Data Architecture & State Flow

### 5.1. The Columnar Approach
To handle 100k+ rows without lag, data is stored in **Columnar Format** (Float64Arrays). 
*   Calculations (Moving Averages, Sharpe Ratios) are performed using vectorized operations.
*   DuckDB-Wasm handles complex joins (e.g., *“Show me the average RSI of all winning trades in the Graveyard”*).

### 5.2. State Segregation
*   **Global Store (Zustand):** `activeDataset`, `filteredTradeIds`, `currentRegime`, `userSettings`.
*   **Ephemeral Bus (Mitt/EventTarget):** `crosshair_move`, `chart_sync_scroll`, `teleport_trigger`.
    *   *Reason:* This prevents React from re-rendering the entire DOM tree 60 times per second during a mouse move.

---

## 6. UX/UI Principles
*   **Layout:** "Bento Box" grid system. Panels are resizable and collapsible.
*   **Latency:** Interaction latency must stay under 16ms (60fps).
*   **Command Palette:** `Cmd+K` for power users to navigate trades by ID, date, or metric (e.g., "Jump to largest loser").
*   **Visual Hierarchy:** High-contrast for critical signals; muted tones for background data.
